1: An apple is a sweet, edible fruit produced by an apple tree (Malus domestica).
2: Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus.
3: The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today.
4: Apples have been grown for thousands of years in Asia and Europe and were brought to North America by European colonists.
5: Apples have religious and mythological significance in many cultures, including Norse, Greek and European Christian traditions.
6: Apple trees are large if grown from seed.
7: Generally, apple cultivars are propagated by grafting onto rootstocks, which control the size of the resulting tree.
8: There are more than 7,500 known cultivars of apples, resulting in a range of desired characteristics.
9: Different cultivars are bred for various tastes and use, including cooking, eating raw and cider production.
10: Trees and fruit are prone to a number of fungal, bacterial and pest problems, which can be controlled by a number of organic and non-organic means.
11: In 2010, the fruit's genome was sequenced as part of research on disease control and selective breeding in apple production.
12: Worldwide production of apples in 2017 was 83.1 million tonnes, with China accounting for half of the total.
